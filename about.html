Introduction

My name is Yulia Kirienko (she/her). I am a Software Developer in Vancouver, BC. I am passionate about using technology as a solution to real-life problems.

I have recently graduated from Web an Mobile App Design and Development program at Langara College. During my studies, I have learned many new skills, not only technical, but people skills as well. I have learned how to work in a versatile team, where the members have different backgrounds. I have also learned how to work independently and how to manage my time effectively. As a team of designers and developers, we created several projects that offered a solution to some real-life problem we had identified. We mostly worked remotely in an agile environment. I have been able to refine and strengthen my public speaking skills and re-discover my aptitude for presenting.

Why Software Development

After working in the hotels industry for several years, I I had grown to realize that I needed something else in my career. While hospitality industry always puts people first, there are still some administrative procedures and tasks that need to be completed. With that, I have discovered in myself a passion for examining, analyzing procedures and looking for ways to make them more efficient. Leaving technology do tedious and repetitive tasks, we can have more time for things that matter the most - people.

In the search of new career avenues I have stumbled upon web development, which, to my surprise, quickly enticed me. One of the main reasons, why I decided to take a leap and try myself in Software Development, is the ability and skills that allow me create tools, which help people and workplaces achieve their goals with less effort and time.

Background

Being originally from Russia, I have received my Bachelor's Degree in International Relations there. That education program allowed me discover and develop one of the most important skills, that define me to this day - critical thinking. Approaching every task scrupulously, understanding its purpose and importance, allowed me to find great motivation in completing it successfully. At the same time, if I find an opportunity to improve the process, I gladly take it, sharing my ideas and propositions with my team.

After graduating university in 2011, I moved to Canada. Back then I was sure hospitality was my passion. After taking some English courses, I started Hospitality Management Diploma Program at the SELC Career College in Vancouver, BC. After working in the industry for several years, I came to understanding that other, non-hospitality, aspects of each job intrigued me more. Hence, I have started exploring other career options by doing some research, taking courses.
